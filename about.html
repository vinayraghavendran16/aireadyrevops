How the framework was built

The AI-Ready RevOps Framework didn't start as a methodology. It started as a recurring pattern in client engagements.

Across thirteen years of Salesforce architecture, RevOps consulting, and integration work, the same conversation kept happening with different companies. Leadership had bought AI tools. The tools were producing outputs. Nobody trusted them. The board was asking why the AI investment wasn't moving the number, and the RevOps team was being asked to fix it — but couldn't articulate what specifically was broken.

Generic audits weren't working. They diagnosed too broadly and prescribed too vaguely. The output was a deck and a recommendation to "improve data quality." Six months later, nothing had changed.

The framework is the answer to that pattern: a quantified, AI-readiness-specific scoring model with 24 named criteria, a defensible math, and a remediation pathway tied to specific dimensions. It's the audit I wished I had to hand to executives years earlier.

Why independent

The big consulting firms have armies of associates and scoped engagements that start at $200K. The boutiques are full-service and want to embed for six months. Neither is what most companies need when the question is "is the foundation ready for the AI we already bought?"

Independent means the audit is delivered by the architect, not handed to a junior. It means the methodology is owned end-to-end. And it means pricing reflects productization — fixed scope, fixed price, fixed timelines — not hourly billing.
